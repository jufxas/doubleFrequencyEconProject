Kevin Diggs
Kevin Diggs is a 23-year-old graduate of Princeton University. He earned a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering within his four years of attending the school and had taken AP Calculus, AP Computer Science, and AP Chemistry in his high school, Frank J. Cicarell Elizabeth High School, and in college. Kevin was a salutatorian in his AP classes in college and had dreamed of starting his own company to where he could put his education to use. Throughout his 4 years of college, along with studying for the classes he was taking, he would also research, blueprint, and discover what was needed to start the headphone manufacturing company of his dreams. When Kevin had graduated college, he began his job hunt and after one month, was able to maintain a job as electronics engineer for Goodlite Product Inc., making $21 an hour. While Kevin was working at his job, whenever he had spare time, he would build prototypes of potential wired and wireless headphones and earbuds. After a year of working at his job, Kevin quit so that he could devote the majority of his time working on constructing a pair of headphones that he deemed perfect. Kevin spent 5 months straight on this task and in total, Kevin had made over 120 blueprints and 50 prototypes/models of headphones. Five months prior to the official start of Double Frequency, Kevin joined a team of people with similar mindsets so that he could start the company and produce wired/wireless earbuds and headphones. Four months later, the team was ready to start the manufacture of the product.
Angel Sosa
Angel Sosa was not able to enroll into college due to the poverty his family and he were facing. He took up 3 jobs in order to provide and maintain his family needs, which made him motivated to pursue a higher education in engineering in hopes of starting a company and getting rich. After 6 years of community college classes pertaining to mechanical engineering, Angel was approached by his high school friend Kevin Diggs while out eating. After talking about what they did after high school, Kevin offered Angel an opportunity to help start-up a headphone company named Double Frequency with him. Angel quickly accepted with hopes of achieving his dreams of wealth and supporting his family, and it paid off. He joined the team as the Quality Control manager. The team of Jazlyn, Unique, Kevin, and Angel, quickly drew up business propositions and possible prototypes of their future product. After months of deliberation, their product was ready to hit the market and be released to the world.
Unique Acevedo
Unique Acevedo tried his hardest to live up to his name his entire life: Unique. After joining many social clubs throughout his high school years, taking many AP classes, and participating in hundreds of community organized events, Unique found that helping and informing others was his gift. Unique graduated from the MIT Sloan School of Management with a bachelor's degree in marketing. After college, Unique approached a variety of jobs: a business teacher, a social media manager, a reporter, etc. All in all, none of them seem to fit his wants and needs. However, everything changed the day that Kevin Diggs, an old student, an old friend from Unique’s AP Calculus class back in high school, approached him at one of the community’s social events. He explained to Unique that he was starting his own business and wanted his social skills and marketing experience on his team. A lightbulb flashed above Unique’s head; he knew this was the opportunity he’s been dreaming of for so long. Business marketing was perfect for Unique, considering he had a degree in marketing after all. He would be their Marketing Manager. After their entire team was assembled, and 4 months of hard work and planning had passed, their business had been ready and would be spread across the world. Their company, Double Frequency, would not stop for anyone.
Jazlyn Sacoto
Jazlyn Sacoto lived in a normal middle-class household until one day there was a fire that burned down the house and they were left with minimum items. Jazlyn began to work 2 jobs on the weekdays and another full-time job on the weekends to help her family pull through. She had to balance out school and work which was very stressful. She took as many AP classes as allowed so she can go to a good college, and in the end, it was all worth it. She made it into community college with a scholarship and studied business and accounting for four years and graduated with a master’s degree. She looked for careers within that field, but nothing seemed right. One day she went out to a job convention and happened to bump into Kevin, who she used to have some of her AP classes with. They caught up on life and Kevin then offered Jazlyn an opportunity to join him in a wireless headphone startup as the accountant. Jazlyn gladly took the opportunity and saw it as a sign. It grew into a wonderful team including Kevin Diggs, Angel Sosa, and Unique Acevedo. A few months after the product was ready to be put on the market. Jazlyn and her team were successful, and Jazlyn was able to give her family the life they deserved, and it was worth everything.​
